Whoami $$\bar{x} := \bigg(\prod_{i=0}^N x_i\bigg)^{1 / N}$$ $$\log \bar{x} = \frac{1}{N} \bigg(\sum_{i=0}^N \log x_i\bigg),$$ i.e., the logarithm of the geometric mean of a variable is given by the arithmetic mean of the logarithm of the variable.

For continuously compounding returns, we model the price of the asset as a function of time \(\hat{p}(t)\). $$\hat{p}(t) = I \cdot \exp(\bar{r} \cdot t),$$ Where \(I\) is the initial investment and \(\bar{r}\) is the average rate of return.

It depends on what you want the mean to be. If you're trying to capture multiplicative relationships, e.g., returns (which compound) or exchange rate fluctuations (which have a multiplicative effect on the asset value), then the geometric mean is superior.
